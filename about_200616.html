Doug Lebda

The inspiration behind Tykoon came to Doug when he was trying to get his oldest daughter excited about earning an allowance while also saving, spending, and giving in a balanced way. He tried dividing her money in three plastic bags and hanging a chore chart on the refrigerator. He was also watching her "work" online to earn virtual money to buy virtual goods. Doug decided to channel his frustration into the vision to start Tykoon. His dream is that Tykoon will empower a new generation of children who grow up financially responsible, motivated, and in control of their money.

Doug has a passion for driving innovation and creating transformative business models that empower consumers.

After his experience buying a house in the mid-1990's, Doug saw a real need in the mortgage lending market for a third party to help make the mortgage process easier for consumers. He founded LendingTree.com in June 1996 and revolutionized how consumers shop for loans, and recently helped its 30 millionth customer through the mortgage process. LendingTree went public in 2000, was purchased by IAC in 2003, and spun out from IAC as a separate public company in 2008. From 2005 to 2008, Doug also served as IAC's President and COO, leading the company through a transition from a holding company to a unified operating company.

In 2010, Doug launched the LendingTree Foundation which empowers individuals in their personal financial lives through education, action and support by matching clients with personal financial coaches. He's also a former Trustee for the Darden School Foundation and a former Board member of the Bucknell Alumni Board.

In addition to LendingTree and his non-profit work, Doug helps lead other companies that are transforming the way consumers transact online. He's the Chairman of the Board of Recyclebank (recently named one of the 50 most innovative companies in the world) and is an active mentor and angel investor. In his free time, he enjoys time with his 3 daughters, is an avid runner and enjoys an occasional round of golf. Doug has also completed two ironman triathlons, and recently summited Mount Rainier.

Mark Bruinooge

Mark is a proud husband and a father to two sons. After being shocked by a rather large credit card charge for online 'apps' downloaded by his sons, Mark decided he needed to build a better way to teach his kids the values of earning, saving, giving and spending money, in a way that is fun and engaging. With a common interest and shared vision, Mark teamed up with Doug Lebda to help kids and families improve their lives through an innovative digital platform.

Mark has broad experience in launching start-ups and developing new concepts in the digital interactive, payments and consumer markets. Prior to co-founding Tykoon, Mark founded Seismic Partners in 2005, a business transformation and new venture development company. He led online sales marketing and developed ecommerce strategy at Bank of America (BAC), where he drove significant growth in online customer acquisition and helped transform BAC's use of the Internet. He also advised and invested in emerging ecommerce and technology companies while at Hambrecht & Quist. He began his career at Brown Brothers Harriman & Co. where he worked in the M&A and private equity groups.

Mark and his family love to spend summers in Vermont, share good meals with friends and play with their growing menagerie of dogs. When family and work aren't keeping him busy, Mark plays guitar in a band with other time starved entrepreneurs and dreams of becoming a gourmet chef.

James Linder

James Linder came to Tykoon with 12 years of product development experience in education, local and business intelligence products and services. At Tykoon, he is responsible for building and running the technology of Tykoon.

Prior to Tykoon, James was Vice President of Engineering at SinglePlatform, a startup providing local businesses the ability to manage their online price lists across 60+ publishers around the internet. There he built and scaled their local business website and publisher APIs from inception to supporting 500k+ menus, 10s of thousands of local business and 50+ publishers including Google, FourSquare, UrbanSpoon and the New York Times. Prior to that, he was Director of Engineering at Wimba, and subsequently Blackboard, and added numerous features to their virtual classroom product, such as transcoding archives into MP3 and MP4 formats, scaled their instant messaging service to support hundreds of thousands of concurrent users and facilitated the transition from Waterfall to Scrum. Before Wimba, James was Software Development Manager and Technical Lead at Cetova Corp., a start-up in the financial reporting and business intelligence industry. His focus on innovation, simplicity, and solving user's needs led to high-quality software that regularly raised customer's productivity by more than 100%.

James holds a Masters of Science in Information Systems from New York University and a Bachelors of Arts in Computer Science from Skidmore Collegetwo ironman triathlons, and recently summited Mount Rainier.

Katheryn Dennison

Katheryn comes to Tykoon with three and half years of product management and business development experience at Bank of America Merrill Lynch where she was responsible for implementing wealth management solutions for the bank's international private client base.

In 2011, she helped launch an international product that increased sales advisor efficiency and was the first of its kind to be offered by a major broker dealer. Katheryn is passionate about creating products that simplify and improve the lives of its users. In her role at Tykoon, Katheryn will focus on innovation and will work continuously to develop and improve the ways Tykoon empowers kids to spend, to save and to give their own money.

Katheryn holds a BA in History from Princeton University where she was a member of the track team and currently resides in the West Village in New York City.

Chris Rodriguez

Chris is a Digital and Traditional Marketing professional, with over seven years experience managing all aspects of marketing - from SEO, Email Marketing, and Social Media to Public Relations and Events. He also has an extensive data mining background, having managed eDiscovery and metadata in the Legal industry for over a decade.

As Tykoon's Director of User Acquisition, Chris is responsible for driving users to the platform and optimizing their experience to increase usage and grow the brand. He will develop a data-driven culture at Tykoon, using best practices to optimize all content and marketing campaigns.

Chris has consistently taken companies to new heights with his strategic campaigns and vision for branding and imaging. Over the years, he has managed and consulted for a diverse group of clients including top companies in Technology (Lean Startup Machine, Speek), Legal (Davis Polk Wardwell LLP, Paul Weiss LLP), and Entertainment (Atlantic Records, Epic Records, Universal Music Group, Violator Management).

Chris has a Bachelors Degree from the University of Virginia. He lives on the beach in the Rockaways with his wife and newborn daughter.
